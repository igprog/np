How to create a balanced diet

Using web application Nutri Prog

Creating a balanced menu for a healthy person is preceded by fact-finding states through several steps:

  1. determination of anthropometric values,
  2. determining energy needs
  3. analysis of existing eating habits based on dietary diaries and
  4. talking to the person for whom the menu is being made.

ANTHROPOMETRIC SIZES

Some anthropometric quantities are directly measured (directly), such as body size weight, body height, bands (waist and hips), and other sizes are derived sizes from anthropometric quantities are an immediate indicator of nutrition, such as body mass index, waist to hip ratio, etc.

Body mass

Body weight is expressed in kilograms (kg) and determined by weighing. It is recommended that it is the morning after urination and defecation whereby the person is barefoot and dressed in light clothing.

Body height Body height is expressed in meters (m) or centimeters (cm). The person to be measured the height should be upright, looking straight, bent legs, barefoot and heels resting altimeter. Body mass index Determination of Body Mass Index (BMI) is today the most widespread way to connect body weight and height. BMI is used because of great connections with body composition. This parameter, as well as the degree information can be determined by computer program Nutrition Program. Note: Body mass index is not used to determine the nutritional status of pregnant women and athletes. BMI of physically active people is often greater than 25 kg / m2, but it is important to know that this is not the case about the increased mass of fat, but already about the increased muscle mass. Speaking of the general population, higher BMIs are associated with an increased risk of high blood pressure, diabetes mellitus II. type of heart and blood vessels disease. Its significant increase in later age greatly increases the incidence of ischemic heart disease. It is weight gain is positively correlated with gallstones, liver disease, the postoperative complications of that death during operations. Complications associated with arthritis, especially the hips and knees, are more common in people of disproportionately high body weight. Obesity is associated with colon cancer, prostate cancer in men and breasts, uterus and ovary in women. Malnutrition, on the other hand, is associated with higher mortality due to lung and gallbladder cancer. Determination of fat content The determination of adipose tissue can be performed in a number of ways including use calipers, underwater weighing, DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry), analysis 26 bioelectrical impedance, fat content calculation according to Young Men's Christian Association (YMCA), etc. It is especially useful in cases of increased BMI, when the determination of fat content indicates whether it is an increased fat content or not. Increased accumulation of fatty tissue in the waist area has the potential to occur various diseases (eg diabetes and heart disease). The risk of these diseases in women it is enlarged if the waist circumference is 80 to 88 cm, and very high when it is greater than 88 cm.
